Article 1

Title: S&P 500 companies are more obsessed than ever with AI

Source: Business Insider

Summary:

  • The percentage of S&P 500 companies mentioning artificial intelligence (AI) on earnings calls reached a record high of 36% in the fourth quarter.
  • Tech giants like Microsoft, Amazon, and IBM discussed AI, while companies outside the tech sector, such as Bank of New York Mellon and UnitedHealth Group, highlighted the potential for AI to enhance productivity and reduce costs.
  • Energy companies saw the biggest increase in AI mentions, while real estate firms experienced a decline.
  • A basket of AI tech stocks has outperformed the equal-weight S&P 500 by 19 percentage points since the beginning of the year.

Title: AI use on dating apps rising, 1 in 4 Americans use to enhance profile

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • A study by cybersecurity firm McAfee found that 23% of Americans are using AI to enhance their dating profiles and messaging, up from 15% last year.
  • The use of AI tools in dating apps has led to an increase in romance scams, fake profiles, and inauthentic descriptions.
  • AI-generated content has resulted in higher match rates, with 69% of respondents saying that AI-generated content earned them more interest than their original content.
  • The rise of AI, particularly generative AI, is making it harder to distinguish real people from fake on dating apps, with 57% of respondents saying they wouldn't be able to reliably identify an AI-generated dating message.
  • Nearly three out of five respondents reported coming across fake, AI-generated profiles in the past year, and almost a third said they had encountered scammers trying to steal money or personal information.

Article 3

Title: Google Gemini AI now in more countries for Android users

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Google has expanded the availability of its Gemini AI assistant and chatbot to more countries and languages.
  • The Gemini assistant was initially available only in the United States and in English, but it has now been rolled out to Android users in Asia.
  • The rollout to European countries may be delayed due to regulatory issues, but efforts are ongoing to address these issues.
  • Gemini is powered by the large language model (LLM) of the same name and is expected to eventually replace Google Assistant.
  • Gemini seamlessly integrates with various Google services, such as Gmail, Google Docs, Google Drive, and YouTube.

Title: OpenAI CEO Sam Altman reveals AI danger which keeps him awake at night

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • OpenAI CEO Sam Altman expressed concerns about the dangers of AI and the need to mitigate them.
  • He called for a universal regulator, similar to the International Atomic Energy Agency, to oversee the AI industry and safeguard against societal misalignments.
  • Altman emphasized the importance of moving from discussions and debates to an action plan with global buy-in in the next few years.
  • OpenAI has previously announced plans for an AI ethics council, aligning with Altman's consistent approach to addressing societal apprehensions about advanced AI.
