Title: Could AI 'trading bots' transform the world of investing?

Source: BBC News

Summary:

  • AI "trading bots" are becoming increasingly popular in the world of investing, with almost one in three investors willing to let a bot make all their investment decisions.
  • However, there are risks associated with relying solely on AI for investment decisions. AI is not a crystal ball and cannot predict unforeseen events that can impact the stock market.
  • The effectiveness of AI-powered investment still needs to be proven over the long term, and human investment professionals still have a significant role to play.
  • AI systems are only as good as the initial data and software used to create them, and if the AI is fed bad data, its decisions may worsen over time.
  • There are also concerns about bias, inaccuracies, data leakage, and model inversion attacks with AI systems.

Title: Your Favorite Songs on TikTok Could Soon Disappear

Source: Wired

Summary:

  • Universal Music Group has removed its catalog from TikTok after failing to reach a licensing agreement with the video-sharing app.
  • Artists such as Taylor Swift, Drake, Bad Bunny, Ariana Grande, and Billie Eilish are affected, and their music is disappearing from TikTok.
  • Users can no longer create new videos with official snippets from UMG artists, and previously published videos containing UMG music have gone silent.
  • The dispute between UMG and TikTok centers around artist compensation, artificial intelligence protections, and platform safety. UMG accuses TikTok of promoting AI music and artist replacement by AI.

Title: AI spam is already starting to ruin the internet

Source: Business Insider

Summary:

  • AI-generated spam is flooding the internet, impacting search results and causing harm to users and platforms like Google.
  • Examples of AI spam include AI-written versions of articles appearing on spam sites, AI-generated obituaries, and AI click farmers taking over websites.
  • These spam tactics are driven by bad actors and scammers who aim to make money by using AI to create a large quantity of content to manipulate search rankings.
  • The issue of AI spam is not only a problem for journalists and grieving families, but also for platforms like Google that end up serving garbage results to users.
