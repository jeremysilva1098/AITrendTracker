Can artificial intelligence help you get fit?

Source: BBC News

Summary:

  • The use of artificial intelligence (AI) in fitness apps and equipment is increasing as an alternative to human trainers.
  • AI-powered fitness apps and devices, such as Magic AI's Magic Mirror, offer personalized workout programs, instant feedback, and suggestions.
  • The global market for AI in the fitness and wellbeing sectors is estimated to be worth $7.8 billion in 2022 and is projected to reach $35.6 billion by 2030.
  • While some people find AI trainers more comfortable and convenient, others believe that human interaction is essential for motivation and emotional intelligence in health and fitness.
  • The first gym has opened where people can exercise together, all trained by AI coaches, combining AI trainers with group classes and human trainers for a better experience.

Chief Justice Roberts casts a wary eye on artificial intelligence in the courts

Source: NPR

Summary:

  • Chief Justice John Roberts discussed the promise and shortcomings of artificial intelligence (AI) in the federal courts in his annual report.
  • Roberts acknowledged that AI can make it easier for people with limited resources to access the courts, but also emphasized the need for caution and humility in using AI.
  • The report comes after instances of AI-generated fake legal citations being included in court records.
  • Roberts compared the use of AI in the courts to the use of optical technology in tennis, highlighting that legal determinations often involve gray areas that require human judgment.
  • The chief justice predicted that AI will significantly impact judicial work, particularly at the trial level.
