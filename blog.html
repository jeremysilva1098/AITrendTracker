Title: Here's everything you need to know about Gemini 1.5, Google's newly updated AI model that hopes to challenge OpenAI

Source: Business Insider

Summary:

  • Google is developing a new large language model called Gemini 1.5 to rival OpenAI's GPT-4.
  • Gemini 1.5 uses a "mixture of experts" model for efficiency and has a larger context window, allowing it to analyze more data.
  • In testing, Gemini 1.5 outperformed its predecessors in benchmark tests and showed enhanced performance.
  • Google conducted extensive ethics and safety testing for Gemini 1.5 to mitigate potential harm.
  • Gemini 1.5 is part of Google's efforts to compete with other big tech companies and startups in the AI space.

Title: Nvidia unveils AI chatbot ‘Chat with RTX’ for local PCs

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Nvidia has released an early version of its Chat with RTX, allowing users to create personalized chatbots with their own content on Windows PCs.
  • The chatbot utilizes retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), NVIDIA TensorRT-LLM software, and NVIDIA RTX acceleration to bring generative AI capabilities to local PCs.
  • Users can upload their own documents and connect local files on their PC as a dataset to enable queries for relevant answers.
  • Chat with RTX supports various file formats and can search YouTube URLs and transcripts for specific mentions or summarize entire videos.
  • The chatbot processing is done locally on the user's PC, ensuring speed, privacy, and the ability to process sensitive data without sharing it with third parties.

Title: Sora AI: ‘everyone will be filmmakers’, internet reacts to OpenAI’s new video generator

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • OpenAI has announced Sora, a new text-to-video AI that can create realistic and imaginative scenes from text instructions.
  • Reactions to Sora have been mixed, with some users enthusiastic about its potential for amateur filmmakers.
  • Sora has limitations in terms of missing out on cause-and-effect details and finer spatial details.
  • It is currently not available for public use and OpenAI has not disclosed whether it will be free or have a tiered access approach.
  • OpenAI is actively working on safety measures to prevent malicious use and engaging with policymakers to address potential abuses of the tool.

Title: Google launches Gemma: new open source AI tool for developers

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Google has launched Gemma, a new open-source AI tool for developers. Gemma is part of a family of lightweight open-source models designed to be used by researchers and developers to innovate with AI.
  • Gemma models come in different sizes to address different computational constraints and applications. Gemma 7B is a seven-billion-parameter model for efficient deployment on GPUs and TPUs, while Gemma 2B is a two-billion-parameter model for CPU and on-device applications.
  • Google is introducing a responsible generative AI toolkit and a debugging tool to guide developers in creating safer AI applications with Gemma.
  • Gemma outperforms similar-sized open models on text-based tasks and demonstrates strong performance across academic benchmarks for language understanding, reasoning, and safety.
  • Developers can access Gemma through integrations with JAX, PyTorch, TensorFlow, and various platforms and frameworks, and Google is offering free credits and grants to support their usage of Gemma.
