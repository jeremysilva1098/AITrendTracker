Title: My Surprisingly Unbiased Week With Elon Musk's 'Politically Biased' Chatbot

Source: Wired

Summary:

  • - Elon Musk's chatbot, Grok, built by his company xAI, has been criticized by some users for being politically liberal, contrary to Musk's right-leaning political views.
  • - Musk has acknowledged the problem and stated that xAI will work to reduce Grok's political bias in the future.
  • - An analysis of Grok's responses found that it exhibits a strong libertarian streak but also leans left on certain topics.
  • - It is challenging to completely remove bias from AI language models, and attempts to do so may result in dull and neutered chatbots.
  • - Grok's performance is currently less advanced than other cutting-edge chatbots like OpenAI's ChatGPT. It is prone to hallucinations and can be easily tricked.

Title: Google Assistant is so Pixel 8 as report claims new 'Pixie' AI assistant may arrive with Pixel 9

Source: Android Central

Summary:

  • - Google is reportedly developing a new AI assistant called Pixie, which could debut as early as next year for Pixel devices.
  • - Pixie is expected to be a more powerful version of Google Assistant, potentially offering more personalized and data-driven features.
  • - The goal of making AI features exclusive to the Pixel lineup is to differentiate Google's products from competitors and attract customers looking for advanced AI capabilities.

Title: Open Source start-up Mistral receives $414 million in funding

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • - Open-source AI provider Mistral has closed its initial round of funding, bringing its total investments to around €450 million.
  • - The funding round values Mistral at $2 billion, making it a leader in European artificial intelligence.
  • - Silicon Valley investors Andreessen Horowitz, Nvidia, and Salesforce have all made funding commitments in Mistral's Series A round.
  • - Mistral aims to bring open-source AI models to the world, fostering open research, open models, and open collaboration in the AI community.
  • - Mistral's exponential growth in investment and Germany's Aleph Alpha receiving a comparable investment highlight Europe's presence in AI start-ups.

Title: OpenAI’s Ilya Sutskever Has a Plan for Keeping Super-Intelligent AI in Check

Source: Wired

Summary:

  • - OpenAI's Superalignment team, led by Ilya Sutskever, is working on guiding the behavior of superintelligent AI models as they become more advanced.
  • - The team has conducted experiments to test a method of letting an inferior AI model guide the behavior of a much smarter one without diminishing its intelligence.
  • - The researchers explored the use of supervision to tune AI systems to be more helpful and less harmful, aiming to automate this process as AI advances.
  • - OpenAI is offering $10 million in grants for further advancements in weak-to-strong supervision, interpretability of advanced models, and strengthening models against prompts designed to break their restrictions.
