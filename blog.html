Title: Anthropic says its latest AI bot can beat Gemini and ChatGPT

Source: The Verge

Summary:

  • Anthropic has released the next generation of its AI models, Claude 3, which is now multimodal and claims to outperform leading models from Google and OpenAI.
  • Claude 3 models can answer more questions, understand longer instructions, and provide more accurate results by processing more context and information.
  • The models are less likely to refuse to answer prompts and can give near-instant results even when handling dense material like research papers.
  • Opus, the largest model, outperformed OpenAI's GPT-4 in graduate-level reasoning and showed better performance in math questions, coding, and reasoning tasks.
  • Anthropic trained the Claude 3 models using a mix of internal and third-party datasets, with investments from Amazon and Google, and the models will be available on AWS and Google's Vertex AI.

Title: Here's everything you need to know about Gemini 1.5, Google's newly updated AI model that hopes to challenge OpenAI

Source: Business Insider

Summary:

  • Google is developing Gemini 1.5, a next-gen AI model designed to be a personal assistant and business tool.
  • Gemini 1.5 uses a "mixture of experts" model for efficiency and has a bigger context window, allowing it to analyze more data.
  • In benchmark tests, Gemini 1.5 outperformed its predecessors in 87% of the tests and was able to find small pieces of text in large blocks of data.
  • Google conducted extensive ethics and safety testing on Gemini 1.5 to mitigate potential harm.
  • Gemini 1.5 is meant to rival competitors like OpenAI's GPT-4.

Title: Anthropic claim new Claude 3 AI chatbot outperforms ChatGPT and Gemini

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Anthropic has launched a new chatbot, Claude 3, which claims to have reached "near-human" levels of performance in some tests.
  • Claude 3 outperforms ChatGPT and Google Gemini in several industry benchmarks.
  • The three models of Claude 3, Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus, offer different levels of power and functionality.
  • Opus, the largest-capacity model, displays higher graduate-level reasoning than GPT-4 and performs better in tasks involving math, coding, and knowledge.
  • Claude 3 has been trained using publicly available and internal data, with hardware from Amazon Web Services and Google Cloud technology.

Title: Google launches Gemma: new open source AI tool for developers

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Google has launched a new open-source AI tool called Gemma, which is part of a family of lightweight models designed for researchers and developers to innovate safely with AI.
  • Gemma comes in two versions, Gemma 2B and Gemma 7B, with different parameter sizes to address various computational constraints and developer requirements.
  • Google claims that Gemma outperforms similar-sized open models on text-based tasks and demonstrates strong performance in language understanding, reasoning, and safety.
  • Alongside Gemma, Google is introducing a responsible generative AI toolkit and a debugging tool to guide the creation of safer AI applications.
  • Google is offering free access to Gemma through Kaggle and Colab, as well as providing free credits and grants to developers and researchers interested in using Gemma.
