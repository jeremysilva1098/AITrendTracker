Title: The Apple Car project is reportedly dead

Source: Engadget

Summary:

  • Apple has officially canceled its Apple Car project after ten years, billions of dollars, and multiple leadership changes.
  • The employees working on the car project will be transferred to Apple's artificial intelligence division to work on generative AI projects.
  • Some hardware engineers and car designers from the car team may be able to apply for jobs in other divisions of the company, while the rest are likely to be laid off.
  • Apple had initially aimed to build a fully self-driving car without pedals or a steering wheel but reportedly pared down its ambitions in recent years.
  • The cancellation of the Apple Car project is seen as a rare setback for the company, which had worked on various aspects of the car's development, including powertrains, self-driving technology, and car interiors.

Title: The Morning After: Apple's car project may be dead

Source: Engadget

Summary:

  • Apple has reportedly canceled its Project Titan, also known as the Apple Car, after nearly a decade of development and multiple leadership changes.
  • The company will move employees from the car project to its artificial intelligence division to focus on generative AI projects.
  • Previous leaks suggested that the Apple Car was initially envisioned as a fully self-driving vehicle but later scaled down to a more standard electric vehicle.
  • The cancellation of the Apple Car project leaves uncertainty about the company's plans in the automotive industry.

Title: Apple to scrap electric car project, much to Elon Musk’s delight

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Apple is winding down its electric car project, Project Titan, and shifting its focus to artificial intelligence (AI).
  • The decision to scrap the project shocked the nearly 2,000 employees involved and was celebrated by Tesla CEO Elon Musk.
  • The car team employees will be transferred to Apple's AI division, led by executive John Giannandrea, but there may be potential layoffs.
  • This move by Apple reflects wider changes in the automotive sector, with leading car manufacturers shifting their focus to hybrid vehicles due to high costs and insufficient charging facilities. Tesla, however, still plans to build new electric vehicles in mid-2025.

Title: Nvidia’s AI sector contributes to significant earnings boost

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

  • Nvidia's AI sector has contributed to a 400% increase in data center earnings, thanks to the high quality of their graphics processing units (GPUs) and cutting-edge deep learning applications.

  • Nvidia's software strategy focuses on the integration of multiple open-source software components to create a strong AI software platform, ensuring consistent performance, enhanced security, and scalability.
