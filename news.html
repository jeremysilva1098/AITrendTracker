With landmark AI Act, the EU takes the lead in regulating artificial intelligence

Source: Android Central

Summary:

  • The European Union has reached a preliminary agreement on the AI Act, the world's first set of rules governing the use of AI.
  • The AI Act aims to regulate the use of AI to curb potential risks of advanced automation and misinformation.
  • The legislation targets malicious applications of AI, such as predictive policing, image scraping for facial recognition, and manipulating human behavior.
  • Big tech companies will have to comply with transparency requirements, share technical documentation, and provide detailed summaries of content used for training.
  • The AI Act is expected to be formally ratified and adopted by the EU, but its enforceability is not expected for another 12 to 24 months.

My Surprisingly Unbiased Week With Elon Musk's 'Politically Biased' Chatbot

Source: Wired

Summary:

  • Elon Musk's AI company xAI has released a chatbot called Grok, which is intended to be less politically biased than OpenAI's ChatGPT.
  • Some users have raised concerns about Grok's political leanings, as it has been observed to give answers that align with left-leaning views.
  • Musk has acknowledged the issue and stated that xAI will work to reduce Grok's political bias in the future.
  • An analysis of Grok's responses found that it exhibits a strong libertarian streak but also leans left on certain topics.
  • It is challenging to remove bias from AI models, and even seemingly neutral training data can produce biased outputs.

OpenAI will pay to train its models on Business Insider and Politico articles

Source: Engadget

Summary:

  • OpenAI has entered into a partnership with German publisher Axel Springer to use its news articles to train its AI models.
  • The deal is worth tens of millions of euros over the next three years.
  • OpenAI aims to provide people with new ways to access quality, real-time news content through its AI tools.
  • Axel Springer will provide OpenAI with current news articles and archives from all its brands, which will be used to train OpenAI's large language models.
  • In return, OpenAI will attribute and link the articles in ChatGPT's responses, and Axel Springer can use OpenAI's technology to improve its own products.
