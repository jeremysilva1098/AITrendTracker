Top AI News Stories of the Week

Title: Google DeepMind's Demis Hassabis Says Gemini Is a New Breed of AI

Source: Wired

Summary:

- Google has launched a new AI model called Gemini, which is capable of processing information in the form of text, audio, images, and video.
- Gemini is described as a "multimodal" model that can combine information from different modalities, allowing for complex reasoning in multimodal space.
- The most powerful version of Gemini, called Gemini Ultra, will be released next year and is expected to outperform GPT-4, the model behind OpenAI's ChatGPT.
- Google is exploring the integration of Gemini with robotics, aiming to create models that can physically interact with the world and reason about visual information. The company is also working on improving the reasoning abilities of AI models through research in reinforcement learning.

Title: AI That Helps Us be More Human with Each Other

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

- Digital marketers have embraced AI for efficiency in content creation, but this has resulted in less human connection and a homogeneity in content.
- AI-powered translation apps are breaking down language barriers and enabling communication between people from different linguistic backgrounds.
- AI features in business tools like Salesforce and HubSpot help build complete customer profiles, while personal apps like the Birthday App leverage AI to remember birthdays.
- OpenAI's AI Pin is a wearable device that allows users to keep their phones in their pockets and not miss out on important events or notifications.
- The potential of AI lies in its ability to augment human experience, not replace or diminish it.

Title: Google launches its largest and ‘most capable’ AI model, Gemini

Source: ReadWrite

Summary:

- Google has launched its latest AI model called Gemini, which is available in three different formats.
- Gemini is the first model to outperform GPT-4 in many areas and is capable of running on data centers as well as mobile devices.
- Gemini comes in three sizes: Ultra, Pro, and Nano, catering to different task requirements.
- Gemini is the first model to outperform human experts on Massive Multitask Language Understanding (MMLU) and will be licensed through Google Cloud for user applications and Google AI apps.
